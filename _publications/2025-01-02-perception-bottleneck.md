Chart understanding is a critical task for Vision-Language Models (VLMs), yet existing models often struggle with perceptual errors when interpreting complex visual data. This paper identifies and analyzes the perception bottleneck that limits VLM performance on chart understanding tasks. We introduce a diagnostic dataset and propose several architectural and training improvements to alleviate this bottleneck. Experiments demonstrate that our approach significantly enhances chart comprehension accuracy and robustness across multiple benchmarks. The code and dataset are released to support future research.
