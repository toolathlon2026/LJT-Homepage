Understanding and controlling the truthfulness of large language models (LLMs) is crucial for their safe deployment. This work identifies a low-dimensional hyperplane in the activation space of LLMs that consistently separates truthful from untruthful statements across diverse models and domains. We show that this "truthfulness hyperplane" is universal—it generalizes across models, tasks, and even unseen data. Leveraging this discovery, we develop methods to steer model generations toward more truthful outputs and to detect potential hallucinations. Experiments on multiple truthfulness benchmarks confirm the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed approach.
