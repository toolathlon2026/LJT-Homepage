This paper introduces SynLogic, a framework for generating large-scale, verifiable reasoning data that can be used to train language models for logical reasoning tasks. The synthesized data is designed to be both diverse and verifiable, enabling models to learn robust reasoning capabilities. The method scales to produce billions of reasoning examples, and experiments show that models trained with SynLogic data achieve significant improvements on a range of logical reasoning benchmarks. The code and data are publicly available to foster further research.
